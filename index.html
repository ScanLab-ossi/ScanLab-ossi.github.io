Osnat (Ossi) Mokryn

Associate Professor, Department of Information Systems, University of Haifa. Director, Social, AI & Networks (SCAN) Lab.

Email: omokryn [at] is.haifa.ac.il

Office: Amir 321

Hanamal 65, Haifa, Israel

I am an Associate Professor in the Department of Information Systems at the University of Haifa, where I lead the Social, AI, and Networks (SCAN) Lab. My research examines how expectations shape both human–AI interaction and data interpretation. I study human–AI teaming in ideation and decision-making settings — including collaboration under stress in high-stakes contexts such as emergency response — and I develop computational and visualization methods for surfacing absences in data: what is missing, underrepresented, or unexpectedly absent relative to what should be there. My work connects human-centered AI, decision support, and absence-aware data analysis.

Starting in 2026, I chair the ACM Intelligent User Interfaces (IUI) Steering Committee. I also chair the M.Sc. program in Human–Computer Interaction in my department. From 2022 to 2024 I was a Visiting Scholar at the Harvard John A. Paulson School of Engineering and Applied Sciences. I hold a Ph.D. in Computer Science from the Hebrew University of Jerusalem, and an M.Sc. and B.Sc. in Computer Engineering from the Technion – Israel Institute of Technology. My research has been funded by the Israel Science Foundation, the Israel Ministry of Science, and industry partners.

My group’s research spans four connected threads: human–AI teaming, learning via surprisability, computational human behavior modeling, and temporal networks. We release code and data for most of our projects. If you are a prospective student interested in these areas, please see the lab page and get in touch.
