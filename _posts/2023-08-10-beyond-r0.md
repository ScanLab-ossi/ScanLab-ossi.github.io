In 2019, in the middle of an Israel Science Foundation–funded effort to quantify the effects of external events on interaction networks, the COVID-19 pandemic arrived. We adapted our **temporal interaction-driven contagion model** to assess individual transmission risk, evaluate social-distancing policies, and analyze competition among viral strains. This line of work appeared in *Scientific Reports* and the *Journal of Biomedical Informatics*; see the [Temporal Networks](/projects/) thread and the [publications](/publications/) page.
