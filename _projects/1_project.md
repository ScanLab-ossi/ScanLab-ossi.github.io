My research and teaching explore how generative AI transforms **human–AI teaming**, with a focus on creativity, decision-making, occupational identity, and ethical design. I study human–AI collaboration across multiple domains: how AI augments group ideation in remote work, how creative communities such as fanfiction navigate authorship and authenticity, and how AI-supported decision tools can foster trust in high-stakes fields such as emergency medical services.

I also examine how AI challenges and reshapes professional identity and agency — as in software engineering, where workers must protect their autonomy, competence, and tacit knowledge. Through my course on ethical sociotechnical design, I engage students with the ethical challenges of designing AI systems that affect human creativity, labor, and community life. Across research and education, I aim to advance human-centered principles for the ethical, empowering integration of AI into complex sociotechnical environments.
