I study what's missing. Most machine learning hunts for the patterns that show up in data; my work asks the opposite question — which *expected* signals are absent, and what does their absence reveal? In 2020 I introduced **Learning via Surprisability**, an information-theoretic method built on a simple insight: a missing feature can be as informative as a present one. It uncovers who wrote a text, flags impersonation, and surfaces anomalies in everything from immune-system data to high-dimensional time series — efficiently, and without labels.

Two other threads run through my lab, **SCAN (Social, AI &amp; Networks)**: how generative AI reshapes human creativity, trust, and teamwork when people and machines create together; and how the *timing* of real-world interactions drives contagion, popularity, and collective behavior.

I chair the [ACM IUI Steering Committee](https://iui.acm.org/steering/), serve as an Associate Editor at *ACM Transactions on Interactive Intelligent Systems*, and was a Visiting Scholar at [Harvard's School of Engineering and Applied Sciences](https://seas.harvard.edu/). I hold a Ph.D. in Computer Science from the Hebrew University of Jerusalem, and an M.Sc. and B.Sc. from the Technion. Prospective students: please see the [lab page](/people/) and get in touch.
