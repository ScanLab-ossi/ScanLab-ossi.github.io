I am an Associate Professor in the Department of Information Systems at the [University of Haifa](https://www.haifa.ac.il/?lang=en), where I lead the **Social, AI, and Networks (SCAN) Lab**. My research examines how *expectations* shape both human–AI interaction and data interpretation. I study human–AI teaming in ideation and decision-making settings — including collaboration under stress in high-stakes contexts such as emergency response — and I develop computational and visualization methods for surfacing **absences** in data: what is missing, underrepresented, or unexpectedly absent relative to what should be there. My work connects human-centered AI, decision support, and absence-aware data analysis.

Starting in 2026, I chair the [ACM Intelligent User Interfaces (IUI) Steering Committee](https://iui.acm.org/steering/). I also chair the M.Sc. program in Human–Computer Interaction in my department. From 2022 to 2024 I was a Visiting Scholar at the [Harvard John A. Paulson School of Engineering and Applied Sciences](https://seas.harvard.edu/). I hold a Ph.D. in Computer Science from the Hebrew University of Jerusalem, and an M.Sc. and B.Sc. in Computer Engineering from the Technion – Israel Institute of Technology. My research has been funded by the Israel Science Foundation, the Israel Ministry of Science, and industry partners.

My group's [research](/projects/) spans four connected threads: **human–AI teaming**, **learning via surprisability**, **computational human behavior modeling**, and **temporal networks**. We release [code and data](https://github.com/ScanLab-ossi) for most of our projects. If you are a prospective student interested in these areas, please see the [lab page](/people/) and get in touch.
